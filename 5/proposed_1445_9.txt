None of these visitors was at ease. But being honest people, they didn't pretend to be. None of them had ever seen a person bearing a title of nobility before, and none had been expecting to see one now. Consequently the title came upon them as a kind of pile driving surprise and caught them unprepared. A few tried to rise to the emergency and got out an awkward. My lord or your lordship or something of that sort, but the great majority were overwhelmed by the unaccustomed word and its dim and awful associations with gilded courts and stately ceremony and anointed kingship, so they only fumbled through the handshake and passed on speechless.
