Simple tradesmen, jumped their counters to become extemporized captains, colonels, and generals, without having ever passed the school of instruction at west point. Nevertheless, they quickly rivaled their compeers of the old continent and like them carried off victories by dint of lavish expenditure in ammunition, money, and men. But the point in which the americans singularly distanced the europeans was in the science of gunnery. Not indeed that their weapons retained a higher degree of perfection than theirs, but that they exhibited unheard of dimensions and consequently attained hitherto unheard of ranges.
